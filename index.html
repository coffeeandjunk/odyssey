Environment

Each environment consists of a set of celestial bodies. They are described as follows:

❎

Date

Change the date here to see the status of the Solar System on that date. This can be used to see various celestial events like Lunar Phases, Seasons of the Earth etc.

❎

Point of View

Choose a Planet here to observe the Solar System or other Planets from it's position. For example, select Earth from here and select Mars from "Look At" to see how Mars looks from Earth's point of view.

❎

Look at

This is used either to study any particular Planet like it's texture and colour, or it can be used to observe the Planet from the viewpoint of another Planet chosen from the "Look from" options.

For example, if Mars is selected from here and Earth is selected from "Look from" then Mars's path will be traced relative to Earth's position when the simulation is playing. This feature is useful to observe the apparent retrograde motion of the planets relative to eachother.

❎

Speed

The slider is used to change the speed of movement of the Planets.

Introduction to Odyssey

Odyssey is a virtual imitation of the Solar System. It is a teaching aid for school teachers to help them explain to students various concepts such as the structure of our Solar System, the relative size and position of the Planets, and phenomena like Lunar Phases, Seasons of Earth. Odyssey can also be used as a learnig aid tool students to tinker and play with it to test what they have learnt in class.

The first thing you see when you enter Odyssey is the current state of our Solar System right at this very moment. You can do the following to see it from various angles:

  • Use the Left Mouse Button (LMB) to tilt and change the viewing angle.
  • Use the Mouse Wheel to zoom in and out.
  • Use the Right Mouse Button (RMB) to pan the view.

Pressing the PLAY button on the top left side of the screen plays the scenario and the passage of time can be seen in the DATE section. Increase or decrease the SPEED to speed up or slow down time.

Each menu has its own help section. Clicking the HELP ICON beside them would give their details.
